Glavani Park FAQ

Planning a day at Istria's adventure park near Pula? Quick answers on hours, booking, location, ages, safety, and groups.

Glavani Park is an outdoor adventure and zipline park in Barban, Istria — about 30 minutes from Pula with ziplines, high ropes, and adrenaline rides. Below are the questions visitors ask most before they book.

Common questions

What are Glavani Park opening hours?

Glavani Park is open daily 9 AM–5 PM during the season. Last entry is 3 PM — allow three to four hours for a full visit.

Can I visit without a booking?

From the start of July through the end of September, walk-ins are welcome during opening hours (9 AM–5 PM, last entry 3 PM), subject to availability — call ahead to avoid disappointment, especially when we have a large group booking. From the end of September until the start of July, all visits must be booked in advance; no walk-ins during that period. Use the online booking form or call +385 91 896 4525.

How do I book Glavani Park online?

Choose a package on the Book page or see packages and prices, pick a date, and submit your booking online. For all advance bookings we email you to confirm your booking, usually within 24 hours. Check your spam or junk folder if you do not see our email. If your visit is within 48 hours, please call to book instead.

How far is Glavani Park from Pula?

Glavani Park is about 30 minutes by car from Pula, 45 from Rovinj, and 10 from Vodnjan. Free parking at Glavani 10, 52207 Barban. See location and directions.

What ages can take part at Glavani Park?

Children's prices apply to visitors under 10 years old. The yellow training route at Glavani Park suits younger children with adult supervision. Taller attractions such as the Human Catapult and Quick Jump have minimum age and height limits — call +385 91 896 4525 if unsure.

What should I wear at Glavani Park?

Wear closed-toe sports shoes, comfortable fitted clothing, and no loose jewellery. Tie back long hair and bring sunscreen and water in summer. More detail on our safety page.

Is Glavani Park safe for first-time visitors?

Yes. Every participant at Glavani Park receives a harness, helmet, and briefing from trained instructors. Equipment is CE-certified for adventure parks and checked daily before opening at 9 AM.

Is there food at Glavani Park?

Coffee, soft drinks, refreshments, and ice cream are available at a shaded picnic area at Glavani Park. You may bring snacks; there is no full restaurant on site.

What payment methods do you accept at Glavani Park?

We accept card and cash payments at the park ticket desk.

Can we book Glavani Park for a large group or birthday?

Online booking covers up to 10 people, including family whole-park packages for 4 or 5. For groups of more than 10 — corporate events, schools, or birthdays — see team building and group packages or call +385 91 896 4525.

From July through September, walk-ins are welcome during opening hours, subject to availability — call ahead to avoid disappointment, especially when we have large group bookings. From late September to early July, advance booking is required — no walk-ins. Book online for up to 10 people, or call for larger groups.
