Once you've completed the yellow and blue routes — or when you're already experienced at our adventure park — it's time to head to the Devil's Causeway Course and cross it on a unicycle. You ride a skateboard first, then cross a wooden slatted bridge before the unicycle — the most difficult part of the park.

If you weigh more than 85 kg or aren't tall enough to reach the unicycle pedals, this game may not be suitable; you can walk on the wire instead. Staff guide you through seat adjustment, balance, and pedalling — hold the safety rope with one hand and the seat with the other. After the unicycle, complete a ropeline and a simple Japanese bridge. The giant swing is the best game to do next. Call us on +385 91 896 4525 if you are unsure.

Visitor photos & videos — Devil's Causeway Course

The park's toughest challenge — unicycle, slackline and Japanese bridge

The Devil's Causeway Course is the black-route finale — skateboard, wooden bridge, unicycle crossing, slackline, and Japanese bridge. It rewards visitors who have built confidence on the yellow and blue routes.

The unicycle and restrictions

The unicycle section is unique in Croatia. Weight limit 85 kg applies to the unicycle bridge; younger children must reach the pedals. Call us on +385 91 896 4525 if you are unsure. Allow extra time — many guests pause to watch others.

What to do next

After the black route many head to the Valley Zipline (Big Zipline) or the Human Catapult. Read more about safety.

Planning your visit

Glavani Park is at Glavani 10, Barban — about 30 minutes from Pula, 45 minutes from Rovinj and Rabac, and 50 minutes from Poreč. Free parking is on site. The park is open daily 9 AM–5 PM with last entry at 3 PM, so allow three to four hours for the whole park.

For Devil's Causeway Course and our other attractions you can book online for groups up to 10 or call ahead for larger parties. See packages and prices — whole park incl. catapult €60–70 (children/adults), whole park without catapult €40–50, training route + 2 games €20–30, family packages from €150.

Safety and equipment

Every activity at Glavani Park is instructor-led. Equipment is CE-certified and checked daily. You receive a harness, helmet, and clear briefing before you start. Read more on our safety page.

Frequently asked questions

How much does Devil's Causeway Course cost at Glavani Park?

Access is included in the Training route + 2 games package (all games except catapult, from €20/€30) or whole park without catapult (from €40/€50). See packages and prices.

Do I need to book Devil's Causeway Course in advance?

From July through the end of September, walk-ins are welcome during opening hours (9 AM–5 PM, last entry 3 PM), subject to availability — call ahead to avoid disappointment, as we may not be able to accommodate walk-ins when we have a large group booking. From the end of September until the start of July, Devil's Causeway Course and all other attractions require advance booking; walk-ins are not accepted.

Is Devil's Causeway Course safe at Glavani Park?

Yes. Devil's Causeway Course at Glavani Park is instructor-led with CE-certified harnesses and helmets, daily equipment checks, and a full briefing before you start. Read our safety page for full standards.
